Architecture – Major Description

Architecture is the art and science of designing and constructing buildings and spaces that are functional, aesthetically meaningful, and socially responsive. It integrates creative design with technical knowledge, sustainability, and an understanding of cultural and historical contexts. Architects shape the built environment to improve how people live, work, and interact with their surroundings.

Architecture Major Requirements (B.Arch or B.S. Arch)

Total Credits Required: 150 credits (for B.Arch, 5-year professional degree) OR 120 credits (for B.S. Arch, 4-year pre-professional degree)

Foundational Core Courses (30–36 credits) These introduce students to architecture, design, and related disciplines:
  • Introduction to Architecture (ARCH 101) – 3 credits
  • Architectural Drawing and Graphics (ARCH 110) – 3 credits
  • Design Studio I and II (ARCH 120/130) – 6 credits
  • History of Architecture I and II (ARCH 140/150) – 6 credits
  • Digital Design Tools and CAD (ARCH 160) – 3 credits
  • Building Materials and Construction (ARCH 170) – 3 credits
  • Environmental Systems or Sustainability (ARCH 180) – 3 credits
