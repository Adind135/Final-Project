Human Resources – Major Description

Human Resources (HR) is the field focused on managing people within organizations to help them perform at their best while supporting business goals. It involves key functions such as recruitment, employee development, performance management, compensation and benefits, labor relations, workplace diversity, and compliance with employment laws.

Human Resources Major Requirements (B.B.A. or B.S.)

Total Credits Required: 60–66 credit hours in business and HR courses, plus general education requirements.

Business Core Courses (24 credits)

These provide a foundation in general business knowledge
  • Introduction to Business (BUS 100) – 3 credits
  • Financial Accounting (ACCT 201) – 3 credits
  • Managerial Accounting (ACCT 202) – 3 credits
  • Microeconomics (ECON 101) – 3 credits
  • Macroeconomics (ECON 102) – 3 credits
  • Principles of Management (MGMT 210) – 3 credits
  • Business Law (BUS 250) – 3 credits
  • Business Statistics or Quantitative Methods (BUS 270) – 3 credits
