Psychology – Major Description

Psychology is the scientific study of behavior, mental processes, and the biological, psychological, and social factors that influence them. It seeks to understand how people perceive, think, feel, and act both as individuals and within groups. Drawing on methods from both the natural and social sciences, psychology explores topics such as learning, memory, emotion, motivation, development, personality, mental health, and interpersonal relationships.

Psychology Major Requirements (B.A. or B.S.) Total Credits Required: 40–50 credit hours in psychology, plus general education requirements.

Core Courses (18 credits) Students must complete all of the following:

  • Introduction to Psychology (PSY 101) – 3 credits
  • Statistical Methods in Psychology (PSY 201) – 3 credits
  • Research Methods in Psychology (PSY 301) – 3 credits
  • Biopsychology or Biological Psychology (PSY 310) – 3 credits
  • Cognitive Psychology (PSY 320) – 3 credits
  • Developmental Psychology (PSY 330) – 3 credits
