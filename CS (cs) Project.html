Computer Science – Major Description

Computer Science is the study of the principles, design, development, and applications of software and computing systems. It combines theory, problem-solving, and practical programming to explore how computers process, store, and communicate information. The field spans topics such as algorithms, software engineering, artificial intelligence, data structures, computer networks, cybersecurity, databases, and human-computer interaction.

Core Courses (24 credits)

Students must complete all of the following:

  • Introduction to Computer Science I (CS 101) – 3 credits
  • Introduction to Computer Science II (CS 102) – 3 credits
  • Data Structures and Algorithms (CS 201) – 3 credits
  • Computer Organization and Architecture (CS 210) – 3 credits
  • Operating Systems (CS 310) – 3 credits
  • Software Engineering (CS 320) – 3 credits
  • Databases (CS 330) – 3 credits
  • Theory of Computation or Algorithms (CS 340 or CS 350) – 3 credits
